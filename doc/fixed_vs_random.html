Random-Effect Methods

In the random effects model, one can still estimate the summary effect size as an inverse-variance weighted sum. However, in this case, the variance for each study is the sum of the within-study variance, εi, plus the between-study variance. The first of these is known from the data, but the between study variance - the heterogeneity, which is usually denoted by τ2 - must be estimated. There are many methods for estimating the heterogeneity of the data, such as the DerSimonian Laird method.
