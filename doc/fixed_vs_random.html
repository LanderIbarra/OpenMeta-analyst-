Fixed-Effect and Random-Effects Models

There are two basic types of models used in meta-analysis: fixed-effect models and random-effects models:

  • Random-effects model - Assume that each study has its own true effect size, which may differ between studies. Sources of uncertainty include both within-study errors and between-study variance - the variance of the true effect sizes. The between-study variance is also known as the heterogeneity of the model.
