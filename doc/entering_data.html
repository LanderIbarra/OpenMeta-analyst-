Entering Data

You can enter data in Open Meta-Analyst in either of the following ways:

  • Type the data directly into the fields in the Open Meta-Analyst window.
  • Copy and paste data from an existing spreadsheet, such as an Excel.

If you have already entered the data into a spreadsheet, copying and pasting is the easiest method. Otherwise, you can enter data directly as follows:

  1. Click the study name field and type a name for the first study. As an option, you can also add the year of the study.
  2. Enter data in the fields to the right of "year". For binary data, the fields are:
    • tx An - The number of events in the treatment group.
    • tx AN - The number of subjects in the treatment group.
    • tx Bn - The number of events in the control group.
    • tx BN - The number of subjects in the control group.

The yellow fields to the right of the data display the following:

  • OR - The effect size - By default, the odds ratio is displayed.
  • lower - The lower bound of the confidence interval at a 95% confidence level
  • upper - The upper bound of the confidence interval at a 95% confidence level
