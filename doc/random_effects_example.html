  • μ is the grand (overall) mean
  • αi is the between-study variance - the difference between the true effect in study i and the grand mean
  • vi is the within-study variance in study i.

In the random effects model, the overall variance is the sum of the within study variance, vi, and the between-study-variance (the heterogeneity). vi can be calculated from the data just as in the fixed-effects case. But the heterogeneity - which is usually denoted by τ2 - must be estimated. There are many methods for estimating the heterogeneity. In the Random-Effects Inverse Variance method, the ratio of the weights for studies i and j is


When the heterogeneity τ2 is large compared to the within-study variances, the ratio of weights is approximately 1, which means that the weights of any two studies i and j are approximately equal. In other words, all studies have approximately the same weight.

You can perform this analysis in Open Meta-Analyst by doing the following steps:

  1. Open the example data file BCG.oma in the sample_data directory.
  2. Select meta-analysis on the Analysis menu.
  3. In the Method & Parameters dialog, select Binary Random as the analysis method.
  4. Click OK.
